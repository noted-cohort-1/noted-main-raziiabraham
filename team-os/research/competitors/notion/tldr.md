# Notion

**Website:** https://www.notion.so
**Category:** note-taking / knowledge base / lightweight database
**Target user:** individuals and teams from 1 to 10,000+

## What they are (30-second read)

Block-based document editor that doubles as a simple database tool. Originated in 2016; the reference point for "all-in-one workspace" messaging. Large template marketplace, deep workspace permissioning, Notion AI as a paid add-on. Dominant in the category.

## Key differentiator

Databases + block editor in one. You can turn any page into a database with properties, views (list, board, calendar, gallery), and relations. Most competitors (including Noted) don't attempt this.

## Pricing (one line)

Free (personal) / Plus $10 per user per month / Business $18 / Enterprise custom. AI is a $10 per user per month add-on on top of any paid plan. See [pricing.md](pricing.md) if/when we add it.

## Our take in one line

Copy their publish-to-web UX, avoid competing on databases, differentiate on BYOK AI economics and multi-provider choice.

## When this matters to Noted

- When a user asks "how is Noted different from Notion?" — see [positioning.md](../../../product/positioning.md).
- When sizing the market — Notion is the reference for what "enough users" looks like in this category.
- When evaluating a feature request — ask "does Notion do this? if so, how, and is it working?" before committing to a design.
