# Noted — Positioning

How we talk about Noted vs. the alternatives. Keep short. Update when the product changes.

## The one-liner

> Noted is a Notion-style note-taking app where AI is built in — bring your own OpenAI, Anthropic, or Google key, and keep the markup.

## vs. Notion

**The angle:** "Notion's shape, without the AI tax, and with model choice."

| Dimension | Notion | Noted |
|---|---|---|
| AI pricing | $10 / user / month add-on | Free — users bring their own API key |
| Model choice | Notion's bundled model | OpenAI, Anthropic, or Google (user picks) |
| Core editor | Proprietary | Blocknote (open) |
| Databases, relations, rollups | ✅ | ❌ not in scope |
| Workspaces, members, roles | ✅ | ❌ not yet built |
| Comments, @mentions, real-time collab | ✅ | ❌ not yet built |
| Publish to web | ✅ | ✅ |
| Pricing model | Free / $10 / $18 / Enterprise | Free (no paid plans today) |

**Where we're genuinely different:** BYOK AI + multi-provider. Everything else is either parity (publish-to-web, editor basics) or a gap we haven't built yet.

**Where we'd lose a head-to-head today:** team collaboration, comments, member management, pricing clarity (because we don't charge anything). Don't position around these.

## vs. Obsidian

**The angle:** "Noted is cloud-native and collaborative-first; Obsidian is local-first and power-user-first." If a user loves Markdown files on disk and plugin ecosystems, Obsidian. If they want "Notion but cheaper AI," Noted.

Not a head-to-head competitor in most conversations — different archetypes.

## vs. Craft / Mem / Reflect

**Craft:** Design-led, Apple-centric. We're cross-platform and AI-first.
**Mem:** AI-native, acquired and narrative folded. Open question whether it's still a competitor in 2026.
**Reflect:** AI-native with backlinks and daily notes. Similar spirit; they charge for AI, we don't.

Dig deeper in [`team-os/research/competitors/`](../research/competitors/) when any of these comes up in a deal or user conversation.

## What we never say

- ❌ "Notion alternative with all the features" — we're not. We're an alternative with a *different trade-off* (AI economics vs. collab depth).
- ❌ "Best AI editor on the market" — unverifiable and a losing claim vs. Notion AI on most dimensions except pricing.
- ❌ "Enterprise-ready" — we have no SSO, no audit logs, no compliance story. Don't pretend.

## When it comes up in a sales or support conversation

1. Lead with the BYOK AI + multi-provider advantage.
2. Be honest about collaboration gaps. "Team workspaces aren't live yet — if that's your main need, we're not there."
3. Never promise parity with Notion on databases, templates marketplace, or enterprise features. Those aren't on the roadmap.
